## Essence of the Report

Accenture's Macro Foresight 2026 report takes a distinctive approach: it's a macroeconomic outlook with technology implications, not a pure tech trends piece. This reflects Accenture Strategy's positioning as business strategy advisors, not just technology consultants.

The core thesis: despite resilience in 2025 (disinflation, rate cuts, AI investment), 2026 will bring modest but uneven growth amid policy uncertainty, geoeconomic fragmentation, and China-led competitive pressure. Companies must shift from "tactical uncertainty-coping" to "strategic adaptation" to a new global economic order.

The report is notable for its emphasis on China competition — not just AI capability competition (like Meeker covers) but broader economic pressure from China's export-led growth suppressing prices and taking market share. This geopolitical lens is absent from most US-centric tech reports.

AI is positioned as the key growth driver of 2026, but with a practical framing: "outperformers will be those most exposed to AI growth tailwinds." The message is clear — move past piloting to scaling, or risk being left behind.

## Key Findings

### Macroeconomic Context
- Global growth remains fragile and uneven despite 2025 resilience
- Clearer policies will coexist with wider divergence
- Geoeconomic fragmentation accelerating
- Financial stress rising

### China as Competitive Threat
- Export-led growth push suppressing global prices
- Taking market share from foreign rivals
- Technology competition intensifying
- Calls for technology sovereignty increasing

### AI as Growth Driver
- AI will factor into "almost every corporate decision"
- Much of 2026 growth predicated on AI adoption
- Winners vs. losers divergence widening
- Geostrategic AI competition intensifying

### Strategic Imperatives
1. **Reassess competitive positioning** — Where can you compete profitably as China reshapes pricing?
2. **Accelerate AI adoption** — Move from pilots to enterprise scaling
3. **Prepare for margin pressure** — Four headwinds: China competition, tariff impacts, weakened consumer, resilience costs
