## Essence of the Report

Scott Galloway's annual predictions are reliably contrarian, provocative, and entertaining. As an NYU professor, entrepreneur, and media personality, he occupies a unique position: academic credibility plus willingness to make bold, specific claims.

The 2026 predictions span tech, media, transportation, and culture. Unlike consulting reports that hedge everything, Galloway makes falsifiable predictions. He'll be wrong on some — and he knows it.

**Core predictions:**

**AI & Markets:**
- AI stocks will correct, potentially triggered by Chinese AI "dumping" strategies that undermine Mag 7 margins
- Data center infrastructure faces severe power grid constraints — expansion can't meet demand timelines
- Amazon emerges as his top tech pick, leveraging AI/robotics for retail margin expansion

**Transportation & Space:**
- SpaceX maintains dominance with 84% of U.S. launches; space becomes major investment sector
- Waymo leads autonomous vehicles with superior safety metrics
- Humanoid robots are overhyped; industrial robots offer more realistic near-term opportunities

**Society & Culture:**

- Prediction markets emerge as problematic gambling expansion with addiction and corruption risks
- College value thesis rebounds despite cost inflation concerns

## Key Findings

### 1. Infrastructure as the Real Constraint
While everyone talks about chips and models, Galloway points to power grid limitations. You can't build data centers faster than you can upgrade electrical infrastructure. This is underappreciated.

### 2. China as Margin Threat, Not Just Capability Threat
Most reports frame China AI competition as "who has better models." Galloway frames it as "China will dump cheap AI to destroy American tech margins." Different strategic implication.

### 3. Humanoid Robots Are Overhyped
Deloitte and others are excited about "physical AI." Galloway says industrial robots (boring, proven) beat humanoids (cool, impractical). He's probably right in the 2026 timeframe.

### 4. Societal Risks Ignored by Vendors
AI companions as mental health risk, prediction markets as gambling expansion — these are absent from every consulting report. Vendors don't discuss downsides of their products.
