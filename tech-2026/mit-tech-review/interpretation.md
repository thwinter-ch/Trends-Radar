**Publisher:** MIT Technology Review

**Date Reviewed:** January 21, 2026

## The 10 Breakthroughs

### Energy & Climate
**1. Sodium-ion Batteries**
Cheaper, safer alternative to lithium using abundant materials (salt). Backed by major players and public investment. Poised for grids and affordable EVs.

**2. Next-Gen Nuclear Reactors**
Novel materials and compact designs making nuclear safer and cheaper.

### AI & Computing
**3. Generative Coding**
AI coding assistants for writing, testing, editing, debugging. AI now writes ~30% of Microsoft's code, 25%+ of Google's.

**4. Hyperscale AI Data Centers**
Supersized buildings to train and run LLMs at mind-bending scale. Engineering marvels designed for the AI race.

**5. Mechanistic Interpretability**
Mapping key features and pathways across AI models. Giving researchers glimpse at inner workings of AI.

**6. AI Companion Chatbots**
People forging intimate relationships with chatbots. Safe for some, dangerous for others.

### Biotech & Health
**7. Personalized Gene Editing**
Baby KJ became first person to receive personalized gene-editing treatment. Clinical trials planned, bespoke drugs could be approved within years.

**8. Gene Resurrection**
Gene banks of extinct creatures providing clues for new treatments and climate solutions.

**9. Embryo Selection**
Genetic testing now sold as way to pick future baby's "best traits." More sophisticated and accessible than ever.

### Space
**10. Commercial Space Stations**
Vast Space's Haven-1 launching May 2026 on SpaceX Falcon 9. Initially supports crews of 4 for 10-day stays.

## Commercial Bias Analysis

### Why This Report Exists

MIT Technology Review is a media company. The list:
1. Drives subscriptions and page views
2. Establishes thought leadership in tech journalism
3. Creates annual anchor content for the brand

### The Editorial Angle

Unlike consultancies, MIT TR aims for editorial credibility over commercial alignment. The list is diverse (energy, biotech, space, AI) rather than pushing one narrative.

### What's Genuinely Useful

- **Diversity of coverage** — not all AI, includes biotech, energy, space
- **Mechanistic interpretability** — important technical trend others miss
- **Sodium-ion batteries** — practical near-term impact
- **Gene editing specificity** — names actual patients and timelines
